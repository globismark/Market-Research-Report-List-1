<p><p>Drug delivery technology refers to the methods used to deliver medications into the body. The market types include oral, injectable, topical, ocular, nasal, pulmonary, transmucosal, and implantable. Oral delivery involves taking medications through the mouth, while injectable refers to drugs administered through injections. Topical delivery involves applying medications on the skin, while ocular delivery is specifically for eye-related conditions. Nasal delivery pertains to drugs delivered through the nasal passages, and pulmonary delivery is for medications inhaled into the lungs. Transmucosal delivery involves drugs absorbed through mucosal membranes, and implantable refers to devices surgically placed in the body to administer medications.</p></p>
