<p><p>Pharmaceutical traceability refers to the capability of accurately tracking the movement of drugs throughout the supply chain. Barcodes are a common traceability method that uses encoded information on labels for identification. Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) is a more advanced technology that utilizes radio waves to track and identify products. Global Positioning System (GPS) is another sophisticated method that allows for real-time monitoring and tracking of pharmaceuticals. These traceability market types play a crucial role in enhancing drug safety, preventing counterfeits, and ensuring the integrity of the pharmaceutical supply chain.</p></p>
