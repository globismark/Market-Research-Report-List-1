<p><p>Bone Mineral Densitometry (BMD) is a medical imaging technique used to measure the density of minerals, such as calcium, in bones. The BMD market comprises different types, namely X-Ray and Ultrasonic. X-Ray BMD uses low doses of radiation to create images of bones, allowing for accurate detection of osteoporosis and other bone disorders. Ultrasonic BMD employs sound waves to measure bone density, making it a radiation-free alternative that is safe for pregnant women and children. Both X-Ray and Ultrasonic methods contribute to assessing and diagnosing bone health conditions effectively.</p></p>
