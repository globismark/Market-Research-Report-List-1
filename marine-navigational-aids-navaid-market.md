Different types of NAVAIDs include buoys, which are floating markers indicating a route or danger; lighthouses, tall towers with a strong light source for nighttime guidance; fog signals, audible signals warning vessels in foggy conditions; day beacons, markers used for daytime navigation; and other aids like radar beacons and radio beacons. These navigational aids help ships and boats determine their position, avoid hazards, and stay on course while navigating in maritime environments.</p></p>
