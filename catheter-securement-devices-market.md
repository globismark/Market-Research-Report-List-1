<p><p>Catheter securement devices refer to medical tools that are used to stabilize and secure catheters in place to prevent accidental dislodgement or movement. The market for such devices is categorized into three types - portal, epidural, and peripheral. Portal catheter securement devices are specifically designed to secure intravenous (IV) catheters in place. Epidural catheter securement devices are used for securing catheters inserted into the epidural space for pain relief. Peripheral catheter securement devices are utilized for securing catheters inserted into peripheral veins, commonly for intravenous therapy or blood sampling.</p></p>
