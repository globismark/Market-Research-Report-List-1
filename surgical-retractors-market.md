<p><p>Surgical retractors are devices used to hold open an incision or wound during a surgical procedure. There are two main types: hand-held retractors and self-retaining retractors. Hand-held retractors require a person to physically hold them in place to keep the wound open. Self-retaining retractors, on the other hand, are designed to stay in place independently, allowing surgeons to have both hands free to perform the procedure. These retractors play a crucial role in maintaining visibility and access for surgeons during various surgical procedures.</p></p>
