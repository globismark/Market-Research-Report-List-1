<p><p>Fermented food and drinks encompass various market types, including alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages, dairy products, bakery foods, and others. Alcoholic drinks, such as beer, wine, and spirits, undergo fermentation primarily through the action of yeast. Non-alcoholic fermented drinks, like kombucha and kefir, are created by fermenting sweetened tea or milk respectively. Dairy food and drinks, like yogurt and cheese, are produced through fermentation by lactic acid bacteria. Bakery foods, such as sourdough bread and fermented pastries, also rely on fermentation for their unique flavors and textures. Lastly, fermentation is utilized in other food categories, like pickles, sauerkraut, and kimchi, to enhance preservation, taste, and probiotic content.</p></p>
