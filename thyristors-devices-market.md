<p><p>Thyristor devices are electronic components used in various applications such as power conversion and control. The market for thyristor devices can be classified into different types:</p><p>1. SCR (Silicon Controlled Rectifier): SCR thyristors are widely used in power electronics to control large electrical currents.</p><p>2. GTO (Gate Turn-Off Thyristor): GTO thyristors have the capability to be turned off by an external signal, making them suitable for high-power applications.</p><p>3. IGCTs (Integrated Gate-Commutated Thyristors): IGCTs are advanced thyristors that combine the functionalities of a power electronic switch and a gate unit, providing higher reliability and efficiency.</p><p>4. GCTs (Gate-Commutated Thyristors): GCTs are thyristors that utilize AC power for their gate control, enabling efficient power conversion.</p><p>Other types of thyristors include MCTs (MOS-controlled thyristors) and Asymmetric thyristors, which are used in specific applications.</p><p>Overall, the thyristor devices market comprises various types catering to different power control and conversion needs.</p></p>
