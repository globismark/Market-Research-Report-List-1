<p><p>Cochlear implants are electronic devices that can help individuals with severe hearing loss or deafness by bypassing the damaged parts of the inner ear and directly stimulating the auditory nerve. In the market, there are two types: Unilateral and Binaural. Unilateral implants are used in adults with hearing loss in one ear, providing improved sound perception in that ear. Binaural implants, on the other hand, are used in adults with hearing loss in both ears, offering enhanced sound localization and speech understanding by utilizing implants in both ears.</p></p>
