Cocoa powder is a refined form obtained by removing cocoa butter from the cocoa bean, commonly used in baking and beverages. Cocoa butter is the fatty substance extracted from cocoa beans, widely used in chocolate production, giving it its smooth texture. Cocoa paste is an unrefined form of cocoa obtained by grinding cocoa beans, often used in confectionery products. Cocoa liquid refers to the liquid form of cocoa, primarily used in beverage manufacturing. The "others" category comprises various forms of cocoa fillings not commonly categorized under these types.</p></p>
