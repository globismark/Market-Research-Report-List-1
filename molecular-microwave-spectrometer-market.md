<p><p>The molecular microwave spectrometer market includes various types of spectroscopy techniques used for analyzing molecular structures. NMR spectroscopy utilizes the nuclear magnetic resonance property of atoms to determine molecular structures. Ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy analyzes the absorption and transmission of light in this wavelength range. Infrared spectroscopy measures the absorption of infrared radiation, providing information about molecular vibrations. Color spectroscopy analyzes the light reflected or absorbed by substances to determine their color properties. Raman spectroscopy uses scattered light to study molecular vibrations. Lastly, "others" refers to additional spectroscopy techniques not mentioned above.</p></p>
