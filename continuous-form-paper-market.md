<p><p>Continuous form paper market includes two major types: single layer and multi-layer paper. Single layer paper is a one-ply paper that is typically used for simple continuous printing tasks. It offers a cost-effective solution for basic printing needs. On the other hand, multi-layer paper consists of several layers, usually with one or more copies or carbonless sheets. This enables the creation of multiple copies of the original document simultaneously through pressure from writing or printing instruments. Multi-layer paper is commonly used in applications where several copies of a document are required, such as invoices, receipts, or order forms.</p></p>

<p><p>The continuous form paper market finds application in two printer types: dot-matrix printers and line printers. Dot-matrix printers use multiple pins to strike against an ink ribbon, creating dots on the paper to form characters or images. They are commonly used for tasks requiring carbon copies or printing in harsh environments. Line printers, on the other hand, print a complete line of text at once using a series of hammers or print elements. These fast printers are mostly used in high-volume operations where speed is crucial, like billing or data processing.</p></p>
