<p><p>Protein fractionation is a process used to separate complex mixtures of proteins into their individual components. There are several types of protein fractionation techniques in the market. Immobilized Metal Affinity Chromatography (IMAC) uses metal ions to selectively bind histidine-tagged proteins for purification. FLAG Affinity Chromatography utilizes anti-FLAG antibodies to capture FLAG-tagged proteins. Glutathione S-Transferase (GST) Affinity Chromatography employs GST-tagged proteins for purification. Anion Exchange Chromatography separates proteins based on their net negative charge, while Cation Exchange Chromatography separates based on positive charge. DNA-binding Protein fractionation is based on the affinity of proteins for DNA, and Maltose-Binding Protein fractionation makes use of maltose-binding protein fusion tags. Other types of protein fractionation methods may also exist.</p></p>
