<p><p>Superconducting Magnetic Energy Storage (SMES) is a technology that uses superconducting coils to store energy in the form of magnetic field. Low Temperature SMES refers to systems using superconducting materials that operate at extremely low temperatures (below -200°C) to achieve zero electrical resistance. On the other hand, High Temperature SMES utilizes materials that can operate at higher temperatures (around -100°C) but still demonstrate superconductivity. Both types of SMES have applications in grid energy storage, power quality improvement, and facilitating renewable energy integration.</p></p>
