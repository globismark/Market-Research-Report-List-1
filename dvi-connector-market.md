<p><p>DVI (Digital Visual Interface) connector market is divided into three types: DVI-A, DVI-D, and DVI-I. DVI-A refers to analog signals used primarily for older displays. DVI-D supports digital signals and is commonly used for modern monitors and projectors. DVI-I, on the other hand, supports both analog and digital signals, making it versatile for various display devices. These distinct market types cater to different needs in terms of signal compatibility and are essential for the seamless connection between computers, displays, and other multimedia devices.</p></p>
