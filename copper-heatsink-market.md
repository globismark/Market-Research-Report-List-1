<p><p>The copper heatsink market can be classified into two main types: passive heatsinks and active heatsinks. </p><p>Passive heatsinks rely on natural convection to dissipate heat. They consist of a copper plate with fins that increase the surface area for better heat transfer. These heatsinks do not require any external power source and are commonly used in low-power electronic devices.</p><p>On the other hand, active heatsinks incorporate a fan or blower to force airflow over the copper fins, enhancing heat dissipation. These heatsinks are suitable for high-power electronic components that generate significant heat. The fan or blower helps to increase the cooling efficiency of the copper heatsink, preventing overheating and maintaining optimal operating conditions.</p></p>
