<p><p>A radiometer is a device used for measuring the intensity of electromagnetic radiation, including visible, ultraviolet, and infrared light. It consists of a glass bulb containing a partial vacuum, with a set of vanes or fins suspended in the center. When exposed to light, the vanes rotate due to the thermal effects of the radiation, providing a measure of the light's intensity.</p><p>The radiometer market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors.
