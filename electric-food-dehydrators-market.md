<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Electric food dehydrators are categorized into three types based on their method of heat transfer: conduction type, convection type, and radiation type. Conduction type dehydrators transfer heat directly from a heating element to the food trays, ensuring even drying. Convection type dehydrators utilize a fan to circulate hot air within the unit, allowing for fast and uniform drying. Radiation type dehydrators, on the other hand, use infrared radiation to heat the food directly, avoiding the need for air circulation. These different types cater to varying consumer needs and preferences in the food dehydration market.</p></p>
