<p><p>The pediatric brain tumor market can be divided into different types based on the specific tumor characteristics. Gliomas are the most common type, originating from glial cells in the brain. Mixed neuronal-glial tumors consist of both neuronal and glial cell components. Embryonal tumors arise from embryonic cells and include medulloblastomas, primitive neuroectodermal tumors, and atypical teratoid/rhabdoid tumors. Other types can include ependymomas, craniopharyngiomas, choroid plexus tumors, and others. These different tumor types require specific diagnostic and treatment approaches, leading to a diverse market landscape for pediatric brain tumor therapeutics.</p></p>
