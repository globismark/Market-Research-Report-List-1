<p><p>Hermetic grain storage refers to a method of storing grains in sealed containers to prevent spoilage and infestation. Four types of hermetic grain storage are commonly used: bag, cocoon, container, and bunker markets. Bag storage involves placing grains in specially designed bags and sealing them to create an airtight environment. Cocoon storage utilizes insect-proof covers that are placed over existing storage structures. Container storage involves using shipping containers equipped with specialized seals and insulation. Bunker storage refers to large concrete structures that are sealed and used to store grains in bulk.</p></p>
