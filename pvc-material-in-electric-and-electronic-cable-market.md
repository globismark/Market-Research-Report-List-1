<p><p>In the electric and electronic cable market, there are two common types of PVC materials: Calcium Carbide Method and Ethylene Method. The Calcium Carbide Method involves using calcium carbide and acetylene gas to produce PVC, while the Ethylene Method uses ethylene gas and oxygen to create the material. Both methods are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and versatility. These PVC materials play a crucial role in the production of electric and electronic cables, providing insulation, protection, and durability to the wires.</p></p>
