<p><p>Molecular scissors technology refers to a set of gene-editing techniques used to precisely modify genetic material. The market types in this field include Cas9, TALENs and MegaTALs, ZFN, and others. Cas9 is a commonly used tool derived from the CRISPR-Cas9 system, while TALENs and MegaTALs utilize proteins called transcription activator-like effector nucleases. ZFN, or zinc finger nucleases, are another class of gene-editing tools. The "other" category encompasses various emerging molecular scissors technologies. These technologies offer unique advantages and applications in fields like medicine, agriculture, and biotechnology.</p></p>
