<p><p>Paraffin inhibitors for crude oil are classified into three main types: polymer type, surfactant type, and compound type. </p><p>Polymer type inhibitors are substances that prevent the formation of paraffin deposits by forming a protective film around the oil molecules. Surfactant type inhibitors work by altering the surface tension of the crude oil, making it harder for paraffin deposits to adhere. Compound type inhibitors use a combination of chemicals to effectively inhibit paraffin deposition. Each type of inhibitor offers unique properties and benefits for preventing paraffin buildup in crude oil pipelines.</p></p>
