<p><p>A resistance bridge is an electrical circuit used to measure unknown resistance values. There are three types of resistance bridges: quarter-bridge, half-bridge, and full-bridge. In a quarter-bridge, only one resistor is connected to the unknown resistance. In a half-bridge, two resistors are connected to the unknown resistance. In a full-bridge, four resistors are connected to the unknown resistance. These different market types provide varying levels of accuracy and sensitivity in measuring unknown resistance values, allowing for flexibility in different applications and requirements.</p></p>
