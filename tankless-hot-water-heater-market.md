<p><p>A tankless hot water heater, also known as an on-demand water heater, is a system that heats water only when it is needed, rather than continuously storing hot water in a tank. It operates by passing cold water through a heat exchanger, where it is rapidly heated by a gas burner or an electric element, providing hot water on demand.</p><p>The tankless hot water heater market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for energy-efficient solutions and the rising awareness about environmental sustainability are driving the adoption of tankless water heaters. These systems eliminate the need for a large storage tank, reducing energy consumption and providing cost savings in the long run. Additionally, tankless hot water heaters offer advantages such as unlimited hot water supply, space-saving design, and longer lifespan compared to traditional water heaters, further fueling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements have improved the efficiency and performance of tankless hot water heaters, making them more attractive to consumers. <p><p>The tankless hot water heater market can be categorized into two types: gas type and electric type. Gas tankless heaters use natural gas or propane to heat water on demand, providing a continuous supply of hot water. They are typically more powerful and energy-efficient than electric models, making them more suitable for larger homes or commercial settings. Electric tankless heaters, on the other hand, use electrical heating elements to heat water and are commonly used in smaller homes or places where gas lines are not available.</p></p>
