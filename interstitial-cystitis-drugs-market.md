<p><p>The market for Interstitial Cystitis (IC) drugs can be categorized into two main types: oral therapy and intravesical therapy. Oral therapy involves the administration of drugs in the form of pills or capsules, which are taken by mouth. These drugs aim to alleviate the symptoms of IC and manage the condition. On the other hand, intravesical therapy involves the direct instillation of medications into the bladder through a catheter. This targeted approach helps reduce inflammation and relieve IC symptoms. Both types of therapies play a crucial role in treating Interstitial Cystitis.</p></p>
