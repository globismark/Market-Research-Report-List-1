<p><p>Explosive Trace Detection (ETD) is a technology used to identify traces of explosive materials. The market for ETD is segmented into various types, including chemiluminescence, thermo-redox, amplifying fluorescent polymer, mass spectrometry, and ion mobility spectrometry. Chemiluminescence technology utilizes a chemical reaction to produce light when in contact with explosives. Thermo-redox technology involves the detection of heat produced by an explosive reaction. Amplifying fluorescent polymer technology amplifies fluorescent signals emitted by explosives. Mass spectrometry identifies explosive molecules based on their mass-to-charge ratio. Ion mobility spectrometry uses the movement of ions in a gas to detect explosive substances.</p></p>
