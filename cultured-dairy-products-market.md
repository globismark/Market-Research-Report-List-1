<p><p>Cultured dairy products, also known as fermented dairy products, are made by adding specific bacteria strains to milk. The bacteria ferment the lactose present in the milk, converting it into lactic acid. This process enhances the taste, texture, and nutritional profile of the dairy products while also increasing their shelf life. Common examples of cultured dairy products include yogurt, kefir, buttermilk, sour cream, and several types of cheese.</p><p>The global cultured dairy products market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue this trend during the forecast period. Factors driving this growth include the increasing awareness about the health benefits associated with consuming fermented dairy products, the rising demand for probiotic-rich foods, and the growing popularity of dairy-based snacks and convenience products. Additionally, the preference for natural and organic foods has further fueled the demand for cultured dairy products.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several key trends. For instance, manufacturers are introducing innovative and exotic flavors to appeal to a wider consumer base, especially the younger generation. Furthermore, there is an increasing demand for lactose-free and plant-based alternatives to cater to consumers with specific dietary restrictions or preferences. The use of advanced technologies in the production process, such as ultra-high temperature processing and freeze-drying, has also resulted in improved product quality and extended shelf life.</p><p>In conclusion, the cultured dairy products market is set to witness a robust growth rate of 8.4% during the forecast period.
