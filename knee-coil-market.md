<p><p>A knee coil is a specialized medical device used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) procedures to obtain high-quality images of the knee joint. It is a radiofrequency coil that is designed to fit closely around the knee, allowing for improved signal reception and better image resolution. Knee coils are used to diagnose various knee conditions such as ligament tears, cartilage damage, and meniscal injuries.</p><p>The knee coil market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. Factors driving this growth include the rising prevalence of knee-related disorders, increasing adoption of MRI for diagnostic purposes, and technological advancements in knee coil design. The market is also witnessing a shift towards the use of lightweight and flexible knee coils, which offer enhanced patient comfort during MRI scans.</p><p>Moreover, the growing geriatric population, who are more susceptible to knee-related ailments, is further driving the demand for knee coils. Additionally, the increasing healthcare expenditure and the availability of advanced healthcare infrastructure in developing countries are expected to contribute to market growth.</p><p>In terms of trends, the knee coil market is witnessing the emergence of wireless knee coils, which eliminate the need for physical connections to the MRI system. This helps in optimizing image quality and reducing scan times.</p><p>Overall, with the increasing demand for MRI scans and technological advancements in knee coil design, the knee coil market is set to grow at a steady pace in the coming years.
