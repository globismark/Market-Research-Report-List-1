<p><p>Peptide therapy is a growing field in which short chains of amino acids, called peptides, are used to address various health conditions. Different routes of administration are utilized to deliver these peptides into the body. The parenteral route involves injection directly into the bloodstream, ensuring rapid and efficient absorption. The mucosal route involves administration through the nose, mouth, or rectum, benefiting from the high concentration of blood vessels in these areas for quick absorption. The oral route involves consumption of peptide-based supplements or medications through the digestive system. The transdermal route utilizes patches or creams to deliver peptides through the skin, allowing gradual absorption over time.</p></p>
