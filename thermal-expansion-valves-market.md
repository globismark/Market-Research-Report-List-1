<p><p>Thermal expansion valves are devices used in refrigeration and air conditioning systems to control the flow of refrigerant. There are two types of valves commonly used in the market: internally equalized and externally equalized valves. </p><p>Internally equalized valves use a built-in pressure equalization mechanism that allows the valve to maintain a stable superheat level. This ensures efficient operation even when there are fluctuations in the system. </p><p>On the other hand, externally equalized valves utilize an external connection to equalize the pressure. This design is typically used in applications where there is a risk of pressure drop or inadequate heat transfer.</p></p>
