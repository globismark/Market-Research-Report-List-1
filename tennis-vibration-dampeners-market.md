<p><p>The tennis vibration dampeners market includes two popular types: the "button" dampener and the "worm" dampener. "Button" dampeners are small accessories placed in-between the strings, usually near the bottom of the racket, to reduce vibrations. They are commonly made of rubber and absorb some of the shock during ball impact. On the other hand, "worm" dampeners are longer and shaped like a worm or snake. They are inserted between multiple strings to provide enhanced vibration reduction. Both types aim to improve player comfort and minimize racket vibrations during gameplay.</p></p>
