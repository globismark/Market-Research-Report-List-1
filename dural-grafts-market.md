<p><p>Dural grafts, also known as dural substitutes, are medical devices used in neurosurgery to repair or replace the dura mater, which is the tough outermost protective covering of the brain and spinal cord. These grafts are designed to provide support and protection to the underlying neural structures during and after surgery. They are commonly made from natural materials such as collagen matrices or synthetic materials such as expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (ePTFE).</p><p>The Dural Grafts Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The Dural Grafts market is comprised of various types, including Xenogeneic Dural Graft, Synthetic Dural Graft, Autologous Dural Graft, and Allogeneic Dural Graft. Xenogeneic Dural Grafts are sourced from different species and are used when human tissue is not available. Synthetic Dural Grafts are man-made alternatives that offer durability and reduced risk of infection. Autologous Dural Grafts are derived from the patient's own tissues, providing a lower risk of rejection. Allogeneic Dural Grafts come from human donors and are used when autologous graft options are limited. Each type caters to specific needs and considerations in the field of dural grafting.</p></p>
