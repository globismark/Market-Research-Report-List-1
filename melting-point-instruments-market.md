<p><p>Melting point instruments are used to determine the melting point of a substance. There are two main types of melting point instruments: digital and analog. Digital melting point instruments provide a digital readout of the melting point, making it easier to read and record data. They often have advanced features and are more precise. Analog melting point instruments use a needle and dial to indicate the melting point, requiring manual reading and interpretation. They are simpler in design and typically less expensive. Both types have their advantages depending on the user's needs and preferences.</p></p>
