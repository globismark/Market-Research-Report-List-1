<p><p>The lithium button cell market comprises two types: rechargeable and non-rechargeable. Rechargeable button cells are designed to be used multiple times, allowing users to recharge the cell once it drains out. They are cost-effective and environmentally friendly, as they reduce waste. On the other hand, non-rechargeable button cells are meant for single-use only and cannot be recharged. They offer a longer shelf life and higher capacity compared to their rechargeable counterparts. Both types of button cells are widely used in small electronic devices such as watches, calculators, and medical devices.</p></p>
