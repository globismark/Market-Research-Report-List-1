<p><p>Thrombolytic drugs are medications used to dissolve blood clots in the body. There are two main types of thrombolytic drugs: fibrin-specific drugs and nonfibrin-specific drugs. Fibrin-specific drugs specifically target the fibrin component of blood clots, breaking them down and restoring blood flow. Nonfibrin-specific drugs, on the other hand, have a broader mechanism of action and can break down both fibrin and other substances present in blood clots. Both types of thrombolytic drugs are used in the treatment of conditions such as heart attacks and strokes to help restore blood flow and prevent further damage.</p></p>
