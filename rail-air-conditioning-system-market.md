<p><p>Rail air-conditioning systems refer to the cooling systems used in trains to maintain comfortable temperatures inside the compartments. There are two main types of rail air-conditioning systems: air cycle air conditioning systems and vapor cycle air conditioning systems. Air cycle systems utilize air compression and expansion to create cooling effects, while vapor cycle systems utilize a refrigerant to transfer heat and cool the air. Both systems aim to provide efficient and effective cooling solutions for passengers traveling by train.</p></p>
