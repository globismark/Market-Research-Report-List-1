<p><p>Unweighing gait trainers are specialized devices used during rehabilitation to help individuals relearn and improve their walking ability. There are three types of unweighing gait trainers available in the market: manual, electric, and pneumatic. Manual gait trainers require the physical assistance of therapists or caregivers to adjust the amount of body weight support provided. Electric gait trainers use motorized systems to assist and control the amount of unweighing support. Pneumatic gait trainers utilize air pressure to establish and regulate the level of body weight support. These different types cater to varying needs and preferences in the rehabilitation process.</p></p>
